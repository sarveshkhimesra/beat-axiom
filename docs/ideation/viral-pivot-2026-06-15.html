Survivors — 6 Ideas (adversarially filtered from 17)

Frame A Breaking fixed assumptions to unlock viral mechanic
The Sales Archetype Card
Result Artifact

At game end, derive a named archetype from the 25 skill dimensions already generated (e.g. "The Disruptor", "The Consensus Builder") and render it as a 1200x627 OG image via Next.js opengraph-image.tsx — currently unimplemented and exactly ready for this. The card shows the archetype name as a hero label, the player's top 3 dimension percentile ranks ("Top 12% on Negotiation"), and one line of the AXIOM quirkySummary. The URL is token-keyed and stable — pasting it into LinkedIn auto-unfurls the card with no upload, no screenshot, no login.

Basis external16Personalities achieves 18% share rate for professional quizzes specifically because the archetype label is positive-sum (every label sounds like an asset) and the card is identity-flattering, not score-ranked.
Why it matters "I'm The Disruptor — top 12% on Negotiation" is a LinkedIn post; "I scored 74/100" is not. The archetype breaks Assumption 7 (result must be about the game) — it becomes a professional identity claim that happens to be verified by AI simulation.
Assumption broken Assumption 7 — result card has to be about the game. Here, it's about who the player is professionally.
Meeting test
Show Rahul his own archetype card first. When the COO posts his type, every AM in the room will want to know theirs — and the card link does the distribution.
Deal Card — Solo One-Round Shareable
Self-Serve Solo Play

Strip the facilitator gate entirely. Generate a UUID token at page load, call mutateGameState with it, and auto-assign the solo player — zero backend changes. The player runs a single round vs NovaBrand, receives a score and AXIOM verdict, and the result is immediately available as a shareable OG card. The entry URL is public, no login, no team setup, no invite. This breaks Assumption 1 (game needs a facilitator) by exploiting that finalizeGame already has no facilitator check.

Basis directfinalizeGame has no facilitator check; the avatar response endpoint has no facilitator gate — any teamId + message works. Solo play is a frontend decision, not a backend constraint.
Why it matters The share card fires at peak emotional moment — the second the player sees their result. 10-minute time-to-result removes all friction between "saw someone's card on LinkedIn" and "I played and shared mine."
Assumption broken Assumption 1 — game needs a facilitator. Also breaks Assumption 2 — game needs 6 teams.
Meeting test
We can put a solo-playable link live this week with no backend work — the share card posts to LinkedIn and every share is a free impression for the offsite.
Caveat: one round = thin signal for archetype. Frame it as "First Test" result, not final verdict — implies replay for full profile.
The Nomination Protocol
Network Seeding & Retention

Every completed game session ends with a structured professional nomination: the player types one LinkedIn handle, and the game generates a pre-filled LinkedIn post draft that reads like peer recognition — "I ran the NovaBrand simulation. I'm nominating [Person X] because I think they'd handle Round 4 differently — and I want to see it." The nominee's name is baked into the URL as a query param, displayed as a personalized banner when they open the game. The seeding event: offsite attendees (who already played) each nominate one person, and Rahul receives nominations from multiple players simultaneously.

Basis externalALS Ice Bucket Challenge: public named nomination + social obligation from named person = 115M raised in 8 weeks. The nominator's status confers obligation — "Rahul nominated you" is categorically more compelling than "the app challenged you."
Why it matters Named nomination is one of the most replicated viral mechanics in existence. It requires no backend — URL construction and a post draft prefill. Viral coefficient is structurally >1 if each player nominates one person who plays.
Assumption broken Assumption 6 — COO is just a viewer. The Nomination Protocol makes Rahul the natural root node of the first wave without requiring him to post; offsite attendees nominate him by default.
Meeting test
Show Rahul the LinkedIn post draft that six offsite attendees would send nominating him — if that appeared in his notifications six times this week, he would play.
Handle validation: player types a LinkedIn handle unverified — add a preview step ("does this look right?") to prevent tagging the wrong person.
Frame B Inverting obvious constraints to their opposite or extreme
Synchronized Deal Day
Result Artifact

Lock the game so a new scenario (NovaBrand brief + AXIOM rubric) releases every Monday at 9 AM and expires Sunday night. Every player that week faces the identical negotiation. The share card shows not a personal score but a membership signal: "Week 23 Deal — played by 47 people." The AXIOM quirkySummary for each week becomes a shared cultural artifact — everyone who played Monday gets the same reference point. An atomic counter on the week key is the only new backend surface; no user identity required.

This is the constraint inversion of "What if the game had no end?" — instead of no end, the game has a weekly pulse. Every Monday is a fresh start that resets the competitive stakes without erasing professional identity.

Basis externalWordle grew from 300 to 13M players in 6 weeks with zero paid distribution. The single shared daily puzzle is what made the grid shareable — "did you get today's?" is a fundamentally different social act than "I got a high score."
Why it matters The Monday card is not "I scored X" — it is "I showed up this week," which is lower ego risk and therefore shared more broadly. Rahul gets a standing Monday moment to post the weekly participant count on LinkedIn, making him the community anchor without requiring him to play every week.
Constraint inverted "What if the game had no end?" → The game has no permanent end state but a weekly reset. Membership in "Week 23" is the identity, not a final verdict.
Meeting test
We keep the exact same game engine — we just lock the scenario to refresh weekly, which means every sales leader in Rahul's network who shares a card is implicitly saying they showed up this week.
The Rahul Challenge Card
COO-Tag Loop

This is the constraint inversion of "What if the COO's result was the ONLY thing shared?" Rahul plays the game once and instead of a generic share button, receives a "Challenge 5 people" flow: he types 5 LinkedIn names, and the system generates 5 personalized game URLs each with a custom OG card that reads "Rahul Kothari challenged you to beat his AXIOM score." The recipient URL pre-fills a "you've been challenged by Rahul" banner in the game UI. Only Rahul's post goes live publicly — the 5 recipients get a personalized entry point, and their results, if shared, reference Rahul's card as the origin node.

Basis externalALS Ice Bucket: the nominator (Rahul) is the public signal, the nominees have a named binary obligation, and the nominator's social status is what confers pressure — same asymmetry, applied to a professional audience where Rahul's status as COO is the lever.
Why it matters Rahul's existing LinkedIn network reach does what paid ads cannot. One post from a COO naming five enterprise sales leaders creates social obligation that an app notification never could. Viral coefficient from the first post is structurally >1.
Constraint inverted "What if the COO's result was the ONLY thing shared?" → Rahul doesn't broadcast his result — he uses it as a proof-of-play credential to challenge others. His result is the authority that makes the challenge credible, not the content itself.
Meeting test
You play once, you get a card, you name five people — your post goes out, five personalized URLs generate, and anything they share traces back to you as the origin; zero ongoing effort required after that first game.
The Closer Score — Credentialled Role Badge
Result Artifact

This is the constraint inversion of "What if there were 1,000 simultaneous players?" When there are 1,000 players, a leaderboard becomes meaningless noise — but a credentialled tier survives at scale. After any solo session, The Deal issues a tier: Trainee Closer / Senior Closer / Master Closer — derived from the existing scoring rubric (margin captured, concession count, round efficiency). The tier renders as a badge OG image at a token-keyed URL. Calibrated so roughly 20% of first-time players reach Senior or above — flattering but not trivial. No user accounts, no cross-session storage. A deterministic tier function over the existing finalizeGame payload.

Basis externalCFA and bar exam pass announcements generate high organic LinkedIn engagement because the result is verifiable proof of demonstrated competence. Credly/Coursera badge sharing is a documented behavior in professional communities. The 20% calibration threshold mirrors the "flattering but not trivial" bar from Interact quiz funnel research.
Why it matters A named tier reframes "I played a sales game" as "I benchmarked my closing ability" — a materially different professional signal. The calibration note solves the "nobody shares a Trainee badge" failure mode: design the thresholds so the first-time player has a real shot at a shareable tier.
Constraint inverted "What if there were 1,000 simultaneous players?" → At 1,000 players, drop the leaderboard and issue a credential instead. Credentials scale to any volume; leaderboards degrade to noise.
Meeting test
The Closer Score turns every player into a walking reference for The Deal — when a Sales VP posts their Master Closer badge, their entire network sees Razorpay's name on a credentialled result.
Caveat: verify that token URLs persist after game state is overwritten by a new game — if tokens are ephemeral, badge links break on LinkedIn (reputation hazard). Write result to finale:{gameId} Redis key with 30-day TTL at finalize time.

Rejected Candidates (11) — Reasons

Peer-First Reveal Cross-token inter-user state doesn't exist; hostage mechanic (player locked until peer responds) converts players to churners.
COO Challenge Drop Entire viral loop contingent on one person posting once — not a mechanic, a prayer. Single point of human failure.
Living Sales Identity Cross-session identity is the core requirement. The Deal has no user accounts; building them is a full product pivot, not an addition.
Deal × 1000 Leaderboard Without identity, same player replays and floods top. Honor-system leaderboards degrade to spam within days of real traffic.
Rahul Challenge Card (5-name) Hard-coded to 5 one-time URLs; viral chain requires each recipient to also nominate, which isn't built in. Doesn't self-propagate. (Merged into Nomination Protocol.)
Daily Rep — Streak Ladder Streaks are definitionally cross-session. No user account layer means every play is Day 1; streak mechanic cannot exist without persistent identity.
Weekly Deal + Leaderboard Optional handle with no identity verification → honor-system leaderboard stuffed within days. Leaderboard half of idea removed; synchronization half absorbed into Synchronized Deal Day.
Spoiler-Safe Recap Grid Wordle grid virality depends on shared scenario; without synchronized scenarios, grids are incomparable and social pull disappears. Viable only as add-on to Synchronized Deal Day.
COO Verdict Card Scales only as long as Rahul manually comments on individual posts. Not a mechanic — a sustained behavioral ask with no enforcement.
COO's Scouting Report Content production workflow for one person, not a self-propagating mechanic. Requires new admin tooling for minimal viral payoff.
Proof-of-Performance Badge Near-duplicate of Closer Score. Closer Score is strictly better: role-specific label, calibration floor, cleaner implementation. Merged.

Next Steps

Run /ce-brainstorm on one of these survivors to define it precisely enough for planning. Recommended starting point:

1. Sales Archetype Card — lowest build cost, highest axis coverage (result artifact + identity), directly enables all other ideas. Build this first; others plug into the OG card infrastructure it creates.

2. Deal Card — Solo One-Round — zero backend work, frontend-only unlock. Pair with the Archetype Card to give the solo player a shareable identity at end of their first round.

3. Nomination Protocol — add as the exit mechanism after the first play. Now the COO-tag loop is live without Rahul having to do anything proactively.

These three together form the minimum viable viral loop: solo play → archetype card → nominate one person. The Synchronized Deal Day and Closer Score are layered on top once the baseline loop is confirmed working.
